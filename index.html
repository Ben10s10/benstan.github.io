About

Background

I am a fourth year Chemical Engineering student at the University of Waterloo with 30 months of hands on experience across research labs, pilot scale manufacturing, and industrial process control.

I recently completed a co-op in the Klinkova Lab, where I worked on catalysis and nanomaterials research involving decoupled electrolysis, electro Fenton oxidation, multimetal nanoparticles, and surface modified electrodes for catalytic and optical applications.

Across earlier co-ops I have contributed to process optimization, including increasing ethanol bioreactor yields by 180 percent, and built workflow automation tools using Power BI, Power Automate, SharePoint, and custom code in Python, C++, and Java.

I am interested in roles and graduate programs that connect catalysis, nuclear engineering, surface engineering, and process intensification with high impact industrial or energy applications.

Current interests

Catalysis and electrochemistry for clean processes, nanomaterial enabled surfaces, nuclear fuel systems, and experimental methods that push toward safer, more efficient reactors and industrial assets.

I enjoy moving between hands on experimentation, data analysis, and automation, then using those tools to design smarter processes.
